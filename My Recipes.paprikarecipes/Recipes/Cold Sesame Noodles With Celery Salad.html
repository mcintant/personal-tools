Cold Sesame Noodles With Celery Salad

Total Time: 15 minutes Servings: 4 Source: Phil’s Favorites, p. 202 - Created by paprikagpt.freas.me

Ingredients

For the Celery Salad:

1 tablespoon sesame oil

1 tablespoon rice vinegar

1 tablespoon soy sauce

½ teaspoon toasted sesame seeds

2 cups celery, thinly sliced

½ cup chopped fresh herbs (cilantro, parsley, or basil)


For the Noodles:

1 pound fresh ramen or egg noodles

2 tablespoons soy sauce

2 tablespoons Chinese sesame paste or tahini

2 teaspoons chili crisp

2 teaspoons rice vinegar

1 tablespoon brown sugar

1 tablespoon sesame oil

1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds

Description

Cold sesame noodles tossed with a rich sesame dressing and served with a fresh celery and herb salad — a quick, flavorful meal for warm days.

Directions

1. Prepare the salad: Heat a skillet over medium heat and toast sesame seeds for a few minutes until fragrant. Stir together sesame oil, rice vinegar, soy sauce, and sesame seeds in a large bowl. Add celery and herbs and toss to coat. Set aside.
2. Prepare the noodles: Boil noodles according to package directions. Drain and rinse under cold water to stop cooking.
3. Whisk together soy sauce, sesame paste, chili crisp, rice vinegar, brown sugar, and sesame oil in a large bowl. Toss the noodles with the sauce and sesame seeds until evenly coated.
4. Serve cold with the celery salad.

Notes

Try adding shredded chicken or tofu for extra protein.
